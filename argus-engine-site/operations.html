Operations and reliability

Make recon repeatable, observable, and reversible.

Operational copy should focus on safe defaults, queue health, durable state, idempotent event handling, and the ability to stop or roll back when reliability budgets are exceeded.

What to monitor

Signals that matter during a run.

Queue health

Track backlog, lease/retry state, duplicate suppression, and worker activity before increasing scope or concurrency.

Persistence health

Watch Postgres connection pools, file-store writes, schema state, and slow queries under worker load.

Message health

Track RabbitMQ connectivity, MassTransit outbox/inbox behavior, duplicate delivery, and consumer failures.

Existing reliability language

Promote implemented safeguards accurately.

The production checklist indicates reliability baseline APIs, error-budget and rollback signals, an outbox/inbox idempotency foundation, buffered async bus journaling, durable spider execution, adaptive spider concurrency, real enumeration and port-scan services, endpoint modules by bounded context, and startup options validation.

Copy should describe those as reliability foundations. Do not imply a completed production hardening story while items such as EF Core migration jobs, transport cutover, authenticated diagnostics, and chaos/performance tests remain open.

Suggested runbook

Operate in small, reviewable increments.

Preflight

Confirm authorization, configuration, queue health, storage health, and worker scale.

Start small

Import a narrow slice of approved scope and verify that Gatekeeper, workers, and persistence behave as expected.

Observe

Watch worker activity, diagnostics, queue state, and database load while the run is active.

Pause when noisy

Reduce scope, lower worker scale, or stop the stack if the signal-to-noise ratio or reliability budget degrades.

Review and archive

Validate findings, attach evidence, record false positives, and store run notes with the engagement record.
