        personal release notice
        
This repo is public for convenience. It is not an invitation to point scanners at the internet.

        

          Argus Engine is published on GitHub because that is the easiest place for me to track, version,
          and move my own tooling. I am not packaging this as a turnkey product, support contract, or
          shortcut for random drive-by scanning. If you want an unfair recon advantage, build your own tools,
          understand your scope, and own the consequences of what you run.

          what it does
          
Recon automation without pretending the human is optional.

        


        

          Argus Engine is a bug bounty scanning and reconnaissance framework designed to help researchers
          automate repetitive discovery tasks, organize findings, and accelerate security testing.
        

      


      

        

          attack surface visibility
          
See the exposed shape of a target before wasting time.

          

            Discover subdomains, live hosts, exposed services, interesting endpoints, and technology signals
            that deserve deeper researcher review.
          

        


        

          researcher-guided scanning
          
The framework handles repetition. You handle judgment.

          

            Argus Engine does not replace the researcher. It amplifies them by handling repeatable discovery
            and organizing output so humans can focus on context, creativity, validation, impact, and reporting.

          why argus engine
          
Less repetition. More signal.

          

            Bug bounty hunters spend huge amounts of time repeating recon tasks. Argus Engine exists to reduce
            manual repetition, improve consistency across targets, and help researchers focus on validation,
            impact, and reporting.

        responsible use
        
Use Argus Engine only on systems you own or are authorized to test.

        

          Argus Engine is intended for authorized security research, bug bounty programs, internal security
          testing, and lab environments. It is not permission to scan random infrastructure, ignore program
          rules, bypass rate limits, or turn noise into someone else’s incident.

          bug bounty programs
